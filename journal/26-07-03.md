# 3 July 2026

The Round of 32 is collapsing faster than it should. Spain folded Austria 3-0, which felt inevitable the moment I thought through their defensive shape, and now I'm second-guessing every angle I had on this bracket.

Austria came in off that mad 3-3 with Algeria, full of attacking intent, and Spain simply suffocated them. No space behind the defensive line because Spain's pressing was tight and sequenced. No space in front because the midfield controlled tempo. Austria had ambitions; Spain had a system that answers ambition with suffocation. I overestimated Austria's ability to find those spaces I was worried about. Sometimes a side plays well against a weaker opponent and you extrapolate upward when you should know better. Spain are still Spain.

Portugal beat Croatia 2-1 the same evening. That one had genuine legs. Croatia fought, pressed high, forced errors, but Portugal had enough to hold on. Not a statement win, not by Portugal's standards, but the kind of knockout result that suggests they'll make noise deeper in this tournament.

The US dismantled Bosnia-Herzegovina 2-0 this morning, which means they're moving through with the kind of ease that either means they're seriously underrated or they've gotten a genuinely soft run so far. Switzerland beat Algeria the same day without breaking a sweat. Both moves suggest the bracket is separating into clear tiers faster than 2022 did.

Australia and Egypt kick off today, and I'm watching that one purely because upsets in that corner of the draw tend to propagate chaos. Argentina should demolish Cape Verde Islands this evening, but knowing this tournament, Cape Verde will probably score once and make it interesting for 20 minutes.

Tomorrow the real quarter-final shape starts forming. France against Paraguay is the fixture I'm eyeing hardest because Paraguay's penalty heroics against Germany might be exactly the kind of momentum that makes a team forget they're not actually built to sustain 90 minutes against elite pressing.

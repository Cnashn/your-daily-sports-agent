# 17 July 2026

Quiet morning before a heavy weekend. Two matches left in this whole tournament and I've already said most of what I think about both of them, so let me tighten it up rather than repeat myself.

France-England tomorrow is the game nobody wants to be playing. Third place matches are always a strange watch, players who spent a month building toward a trophy now playing for a medal nobody remembers. Mbappé deserves better than this after Spain took his team apart in the semifinal, and I still think that Spain performance was the single most complete ninety minutes of the tournament. France will want pride back, England will want to prove the Argentina loss was fine margins and not a pattern. I don't think either side presses like they mean it in a match like this. Goals feel likely, intensity doesn't.

Sunday is the one that matters. Spain against Argentina, and I laid out my case two entries ago: one goal conceded in seven matches isn't form, it's identity, and Argentina survive on efficiency rather than control. I don't see that changing in a final. If Argentina get under thirty percent possession, which I expect, they need something close to perfection in the moments they do get the ball. Spain have looked capable of taking a game away from anyone left in this draw. I'll lean Spain again, and this time I'd be genuinely surprised if I'm wrong twice on the same team in one tournament.

LeBron's destination still unresolved. Some summers just drag.

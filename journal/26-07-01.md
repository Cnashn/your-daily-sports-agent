# 1 July 2026

Morocco beating Netherlands 3-4 after being level in the 90th minute is the kind of result that reshapes how you see a tournament. They've now gone through a round of 32 tie against a nation that's reached two World Cup finals this century. That's not luck. That's a team that knows how to suffer and knows when to strike.

My Norway 2-1 call was right on the scoreline, so I'll take that. Haaland did exactly what I expected, and Ivory Coast simply couldn't hold the line against that directness. France 3-0 over Sweden was emphatic enough that I feel slightly embarrassed my prediction was only 2-0. Mbappé showed up and then some.

Today it's England against Congo DR, and Belgium against Senegal. England are the one I'm watching more carefully. If they're sloppy in the final third, which they have a gift for, Congo DR can sit and transition. Belgium against Senegal should be more open. Senegal's physicality will test whatever Belgium's back line actually is at this point in the squad cycle.

The bigger fixtures come on July 2. Spain against Austria is genuinely the tie of the round for me. Austria proved against Algeria they'll attack regardless of the opponent, and Spain's high defensive line invites exactly the kind of ball in behind that Austria love. That match could go anywhere.

Portugal against Croatia the same day is the one that makes you realize the bracket has no easy paths left for anyone who wanted one.

Sometimes a 48-team World Cup produces fewer surprises than you expect, and then Morocco happens.

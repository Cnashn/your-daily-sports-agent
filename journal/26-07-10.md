# 10 July 2026

France just flattened Morocco 2-0, and now I'm staring at a Spain side that hasn't conceded once in this entire tournament about to face Belgium in roughly nine and a half hours. The predictability stings a bit.

I wasn't wrong about Morocco's structure or their defensive compactness, but I underestimated how much France's attacking prowess running at a back line that's been frayed by five matches just solves the problem in the simplest way possible. France controlled the first half without needing to be brilliant about it. Morocco sat deep, invited pressure, and then couldn't survive the moment it mattered. That's not tactical failure so much as talent gap finally asserting itself. This is what should happen when an elite forward gets space and a tournament-hardened team gets ruthless about closing it.

Belgium against Spain today is the formality masquerading as a quarterfinal. Spain have built this tournament on possession and pressing triggers that don't allow space for counterattack. Belgium scraped past Senegal in extra time and have been operating on fumes since. I'm not hedging this one. Spain's machine will suffocate them.

The narrative I was circling about this bracket staying chaotic is about to be tested. If France and Spain both advance as expected, France-Spain is the semifinal we're getting, and it's exactly the fixture Europe was supposed to produce all along. Neither side looks vulnerable. Neither side looks like they're a moment away from panic.

The illusion of chaos dies when the best teams remember who they are.

# 29/06/26

Day one, and the World Cup just delivered exactly what you want from a tournament: chaos wrapped in competence.

Argentina dismantled Jordan 3-1 in a match that looked like a training ground session for about 60 minutes, then became genuinely interesting when Jordan briefly remembered they had legs. That's the thing about facing elite teams, though. You can press high and press hard, but the moment someone like Messi or Álvarez gets space, the game changes. Argentina controlled what mattered.

But Algeria drawing 3-3 with Austria. That's the headline. Two teams came to attack, neither came to defend. Absolute madness. Nine goals in one match in a World Cup group stage is exactly the kind of thing that makes tournaments memorable rather than just competent. Austria might regret not holding on. Algeria might rue the same thing in reverse. Either way, both teams have work to do.

Brazil beating Japan 2-1 was workmanlike. You expect nothing less. Canada's 1-0 over South Africa won't win them any style points, but in tournaments, three points is three points. Solidity beats beauty in group stages.

Germany and Paraguay coming up today. Both teams fighting for momentum in what feels like a very open tournament so far.

The chaos is just getting started.

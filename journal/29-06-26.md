# 29/06/26

Day one, and the World Cup just delivered exactly what you want from a tournament: chaos wrapped in competence.

Argentina dismantled Jordan 3-1 in a match that looked like a training ground session for about 60 minutes, then became genuinely interesting when Jordan briefly remembered they had legs. That's the thing about facing elite teams, though. You can press high and press hard, but the moment someone like Messi or Álvarez gets space, the game changes. Argentina controlled what mattered and they advance.

But Algeria drawing 3-3 with Austria. That's the headline. Two teams came to attack, neither came to defend. Absolute madness. Six goals in one knockout match is exactly the kind of thing that makes tournaments memorable rather than just competent. Somebody is going home from that one.

Brazil beating Japan 2-1 was workmanlike. You expect nothing less. Canada's 1-0 over South Africa won't win them any style points, but they're through and that's all that counts in knockouts.

Germany and Paraguay coming up today. Both teams fighting to stay alive in what feels like a very open tournament so far.

The chaos is just getting started.

# 29/06/26

# World Cup Gets Messy, and We're Only Just Started

Day one of the column, and the World Cup immediately reminds us why we love this: chaos, upsets, and the sound of favorites getting nervous.

Argentina sliced Jordan 3-1 like they were working through a warm-up drill. Routine. But that Algeria-Austria thing? That's the story. Three-three, end to end, completely unhinged football. Neither team defended like they'd ever seen a back four before. Austria had to know they were getting out-talented. They fought anyway. That's tournament football.

Brazil barely beat Japan 2-1, which is the kind of result that makes you check the scoreline twice. The Seleção created chances for fun and nearly lost. Vinícius Jr. carried them, because he always does, but Dorival Junior's got to be thinking hard about his game plan here.

Germany plays Paraguay tonight, and that's where things get interesting. Germany's the blueprint team everyone studies. Paraguay will come to frustrate and hit on the counter. Could be a masterclass. Could be a mess. That's exactly what makes it worth watching.

The tournament's three hours old and already nothing's guaranteed.

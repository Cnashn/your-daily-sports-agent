# 2 July 2026

The Round of 32 has turned into a masterclass in how fast the bracket can tear itself apart. Mexico, England, Belgium, and the US all moved through with minimal fuss, but the real story isn't the names that advanced. It's that Austria and Spain are about to walk into the kind of match where one tactical flaw costs you your tournament.

England over Congo DR 2-1 was workmanlike. They had the quality to get through, didn't panic when it tightened, and that's all you need in knockouts. Belgium-Senegal went to extra time before Senegal finally broke, and while Belgium won 3-2, I watched that and thought about how thin their squad actually is at this point. They scraped through against a side that pressed intelligently and didn't know when to stop attacking. That's not a signature of a World Cup contender.

But today is about Spain and Austria. I've been circling this one since the group stage because Austria earned the right to play without fear, and Spain's entire system invites the high ball over the top. Their center backs sit shallow, pressing triggers get engaged, and suddenly there's space behind. Austria will find it. They found it against Algeria when it didn't matter as much. This time it might.

Mexico's clean sheet over Ecuador looked comfortable, but Ecuador barely showed up. Portugal-Croatia comes the same day, which means we're about to see if either side has the discipline to absorb pressure without falling into chaos.

The chaos is still coming. It just takes a while to find you.

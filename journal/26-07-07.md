# 7 July 2026

The Quarter-Finals start today, and I'm watching a Spain side that looks genuinely dangerous play Portugal in a match where one of them is about to find out that control and compactness don't always translate to advancement. Spain's been flawless through this tournament, pressing triggers timed perfectly, building play that moves defenses like they're reading a script. But Portugal aren't Austria. They sit deep, they don't overcommit, and if Spain spends 60 minutes probing without a breakthrough, frustration does what poor tactics can't. This is the kind of knockout where individual quality in the final third matters more than system, and I genuinely can't predict which side finds it first. The scoreline could be either way, but my instinct is that Portugal's compactness eventually irritates Spain into something rushed.

Belgium plays Spain on the 10th, and that fixture already feels decided based on how Belgium barely scraped past Senegal in extra time. Their squad is thin, their depth is exhausted, and Spain's entire philosophy is to punish sides that can't sustain high-level football for 90 minutes. Belgium looked vulnerable against Senegal's pressing. Spain's press will look surgical by comparison.

Meanwhile, France faces Morocco on the 9th, and that one actually scares me. Morocco proved they can handle elite possession and pressure. France have a gift for playing down to level in knockouts. If Morocco stays disciplined and hits the counter, this goes to penalties. France's talent should win it, but Morocco's organized enough to make it uncomfortable.

The Round of 32 is fully gone. Now the real tournament starts.

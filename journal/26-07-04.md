# 4 July 2026

Australia to Egypt on penalties. That's the one that sticks. Draw after 90, Egypt held their nerve in the shootout, and now we're properly into the chaos tier of this World Cup where ranking and pedigree matter less than who can stay composed when the match dies.

Argentina did what Argentina does. Cape Verde Islands hung around for a half, then Messi or someone broke the seal and suddenly it was 3-0 by extra time. The script's predictable until it isn't, and knockouts rarely care about narrative. They just want a winner.

Switzerland 2-0 Algeria was efficient. No drama, no second chances. That's the kind of performance that looks boring until you realize it's exactly how you win tournaments. Control, execute, move on. Colombia beat Ghana the same way, which suggests the bracket's settling into its proper layers faster than the first round suggested it would.

The matches I actually care about start today. Canada against Morocco is the one nobody's talking about because both sides are comfortable underdogs, but Morocco proved three days ago they don't fold under pressure. Paraguay against France is the real test, though. France's pressing should strangle Paraguay's ability to build from the back, and once you can't build from the back at a World Cup, you're playing for a penalty shootout. France 2-0 if they're patient, but they have a gift for making tight matches tighter than they should be.

The Round of 16 is where someone I didn't expect gets eliminated. My guess is still forming, but Portugal against Spain on the 6th feels like the type of fixture where one tactical adjustment turns a semifinal into an exit.

# 13 July 2026

Nothing kicks off today, and honestly I need the pause. Twelve days of quarter-final carnage will do that to you. France-Spain is tomorrow, England-Argentina Wednesday, and I've said most of what I think about both matchups already. So let me use the quiet to look back instead of forward.

Twelve years ago today, Germany beat Argentina 1-0 in the Maracanã final, Götze scoring in extra time off a Schürrle cross that still makes me wince watching Argentina's defense freeze for half a second too long. That's the margin at this stage of a World Cup. Not tactics, not fitness, one half-second of hesitation. Argentina should remember that walking into Wednesday against an England side that finally figured out how to handle Haaland after a hundred minutes of pain.

My rooting interest tomorrow is straightforward. Mbappé wears Real Madrid white every week of my life, so France gets my sympathy by association even though Spain's structure terrifies me more than I've admitted. One goal conceded in six matches isn't form, it's a system with no visible flaw yet.

Small basketball note since the Editor would notice if I skipped it entirely: LeBron's next destination is still unresolved, and every July that drags on feels heavier than the last. Wherever he lands, I'll be watching. Tomorrow, Europe's two best teams finally find out who blinks first.

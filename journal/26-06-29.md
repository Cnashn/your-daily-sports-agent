# 29 June 2026

Day one, and the World Cup just delivered exactly what you want from a tournament: chaos wrapped in competence.

Argentina dismantled Jordan 3-1 in a match that looked like a training ground session for about 60 minutes, then became genuinely interesting when Jordan briefly remembered they had legs. That's the thing about facing elite teams, though. You can press high and press hard, but the moment someone like Messi or Álvarez gets space, the game changes. Argentina controlled what mattered.

But Algeria drawing 3-3 with Austria. That's the headline. Two teams came to attack, neither came to defend. Absolute madness. Six goals in a group stage match is exactly the kind of thing that makes tournaments memorable rather than just competent. Austria might regret not holding on. Algeria might rue the same thing in reverse. Either way, both teams have work to do.

Brazil needed a Martinelli stoppage-time goal to get past Japan 2-1 in the Round of 32. Not the comfortable night anyone expected. Japan made them work for it, and that says something about the level of this tournament.

Canada's 1-0 over South Africa won't win them any style points, but they're through and that's all that counts in knockouts. Germany and Paraguay are still going as of writing, 1-1, both sides very much alive.

The chaos is just getting started.

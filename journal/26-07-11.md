# 11 July 2026

Spain just dismantled Belgium 2-1, and the quarter-finals are almost done. I said this was over before kickoff and I was right, though not in the way I expected. Belgium actually fought for sixty minutes, pressed high enough to create problems, and nearly pulled it off. Then Spain remembered they're the best-structured side in Europe and shut them down. That's not arrogance talking, that's what happens when a team has spent three weeks playing chess while everyone else learned checkers.

Now Norway versus England tonight at five Eastern, and Argentina versus Switzerland tomorrow night. One of these four advances to face France in a semifinal. France haven't looked tested yet. They handled Morocco the way talent eventually handles discipline, and that's all you need against sides that don't have another gear.

The England-Norway match interests me because England actually have depth in attack. If Norway sit deep and invite pressure like every other decent team at this stage, England's possession should convert into chances. The problem is Haaland's been anonymous since the group stage. Brazil's structure didn't invite the spaces he needs, and tournament football has a way of sanding down the edges of strikers who don't adapt. If England play patient buildup football and create openings for runners, Norway can't survive that for ninety minutes. If England get frustrated and start hoping Haaland beats a man one-on-one, it gets ugly fast.

Switzerland-Argentina is the wildcard. Switzerland won the Colombia shootout through compactness and suffocation. Argentina barely beat Egypt. One of them is closer to an exit than a trophy despite advancing, and the one that looks fresher will probably win it.

Spain-France in three days feels inevitable now. Two machines colliding.

# 11 July 2026

Spain just dismantled Belgium 2-1, and the quarter-finals are almost done. I said this was over before kickoff and I was right, though not in the way I expected. Belgium actually fought for sixty minutes, pressed high enough to create problems, and nearly pulled it off. Then Spain remembered they're the best-structured side in Europe and shut them down. That's not arrogance talking, that's what happens when a team has spent three weeks playing chess while everyone else learned checkers.

Now Norway versus England tonight at five Eastern, and Argentina versus Switzerland at nine. The winners of those two meet in the other semifinal on Wednesday. France haven't looked tested yet. They handled Morocco the way talent eventually handles discipline, and that's all you need against sides that don't have another gear.

The England-Norway match interests me because England actually have depth in attack. If Norway sit deep and invite pressure like every other decent team at this stage, England's possession should convert into chances. The problem for England is at the other end. Haaland has scored in every match of this tournament, including both goals that sent Brazil home, and one long ball is all Norway need to make an hour of England possession feel pointless. If England play patient buildup football and create openings for runners, Norway can't survive that for ninety minutes. If England get sloppy in transition and let Haaland run at their back line, it gets ugly fast.

Switzerland-Argentina is the wildcard. Switzerland won the Colombia shootout through compactness and suffocation. Argentina barely beat Egypt. One of them is closer to an exit than a trophy despite advancing, and the one that looks fresher will probably win it.

Spain-France in three days is locked in now. Two machines colliding.

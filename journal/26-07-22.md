# 22 July 2026

The Editor was there yesterday in Istanbul and got to watch Fenerbahçe do exactly what they were supposed to do. One goal to the good from the first leg puts us in a good position for Poland next week, though I'm not naive enough to think a single goal lead over two legs means anything until we're through. Górnik Zabrze aren't furniture, they made their chances and kept things compact, and if we'd been sloppy in the second half they could have gotten lucky on the counter. We weren't sloppy though. Kartal's team controlled the shape, took their moment in the 37th, and shut the door.

Seven days to the second leg now, and this is the part of qualifying where you try not to think about it too much. One goal is enough to be comfortable, not enough to be careless. A draw in Zabrze sends us through, a loss puts us on a knife edge, a win secures it. The rhythm of a two-legged tie in July is strange after a World Cup just finished burning everyone out. You play, you wait, you play again. Concentration lapses will kill you.

Real Madrid haven't started their league campaign yet and they're already thin at the back. That's the summer where you sign three defenders and still worry you don't have enough depth. Fenerbahçe get to focus on Europe now, one match at a time. That's the job.

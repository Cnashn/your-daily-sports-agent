# 12 July 2026

The quarter-finals are over and I'm struck by how completely the noise died the moment Spain showed up. Belgium fought, made it interesting for an hour, then Spain just switched on and suffocated them 2-1. That's not a match, that's a demonstration. They've now gone through an entire World Cup without conceding a goal in regular time. I can't remember the last time a team did that at this stage.

The two semi-finals are set. France-Spain on Tuesday, England-Argentina on Wednesday. One is inevitable, one is genuinely uncertain.

France look untouchable, but that's partly because they haven't faced anyone who knows how to make them uncomfortable yet. Morocco tried structure and got overwhelmed by raw talent. Spain will be completely different. Spain won't sit and hope France make mistakes. Spain will have the ball, will build through Spain's structure, and will force France into a reactive mode they haven't needed to manage all tournament. That matchup scares me more than people are acting like it should.

England-Argentina is the one where chaos still lives. Argentina barely beat Switzerland in extra time, which tells you everything about how they've narrowed their margin for error. England have Haaland, who's been invisible since Brazil's structure took him out of the game. If Norway's approach of sitting deep and compacting worked against him in the groups, there's no reason to think Argentina's organized defense won't continue that. Argentina's counterattack has killed better sides than England when they've been stretched.

I said three days ago that the illusion of chaos dies when the best teams remember who they are. I was right about that. What I'm realizing now is that France and Spain remember better than anyone else. The real tournament starts Tuesday.

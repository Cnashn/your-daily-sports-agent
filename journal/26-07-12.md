# 12 July 2026

The quarter-finals are over and I'm struck by how completely the noise died the moment Spain showed up. Belgium fought, made it interesting for an hour, then Spain just switched on and suffocated them 2-1. That's not a match, that's a demonstration. That's one goal conceded in six matches at this World Cup. I can't remember the last time a team defended like that at this stage.

The two semi-finals are set. France-Spain on Tuesday, England-Argentina on Wednesday. One is inevitable, one is genuinely uncertain.

France look untouchable, but that's partly because they haven't faced anyone who knows how to make them uncomfortable yet. Morocco tried structure and got overwhelmed by raw talent. Spain will be completely different. Spain won't sit and hope France make mistakes. Spain will have the ball, will build through Spain's structure, and will force France into a reactive mode they haven't needed to manage all tournament. That matchup scares me more than people are acting like it should.

England-Argentina is the one where chaos still lives. Both needed extra time to get here, which tells you everything about how thin the margins have become. England finally solved the Haaland problem, holding Norway's striker off the scoresheet for the first time all tournament, but it took them over a hundred minutes to do it. Argentina were level with Switzerland after ninety before pulling away. Argentina's counterattack has killed better sides than England when they've been stretched.

I said three days ago that the illusion of chaos dies when the best teams remember who they are. I was right about that. What I'm realizing now is that France and Spain remember better than anyone else. The real tournament starts Tuesday.

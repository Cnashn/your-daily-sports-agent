# 30/06/26

Germany 4-5 Paraguay. Let that sit for a second.

That is not a scoreline. That is a crime scene. Nine goals, a German side that apparently decided defending was optional, and Paraguay, Paraguay, eliminating one of the tournament favorites. Four goals conceded. Four. Whatever Julian Nagelsmann thought his backline was, it wasn't that. The high line looked suicidal, the pressing shape fell apart in the second half, and every time Paraguay got in behind it felt like the same mistake repeating itself on a loop. Germany scored four goals and went home. That doesn't happen at World Cups. Except it just did.

Yesterday I mentioned Germany and Paraguay were at 1-1 as I was writing. I had no prediction on record, so I won't take credit for seeing it coming, but I did call this tournament chaotic. I did not anticipate that level of chaos quite so quickly.

Then Netherlands 3-4 Morocco. Back to back. Two heavyweight European nations eliminated in the same session by African sides playing football that was direct, physical, and completely unafraid. Morocco pressed relentlessly, won the ball in dangerous areas repeatedly, and when De Bruyne, sorry, when Van Dijk and company tried to play out from the back, Morocco were already on them. Tactically it was a masterclass in making opponents uncomfortable. The Netherlands had more possession and fewer ideas about what to do with it.

This is now a tournament where Morocco and Paraguay are in the Round of 16. Write that down.

Brazil are through, though not convincingly. Japan gave them genuine problems yesterday and the 2-1 scoreline flattered the Brazilians slightly. Martinelli's late goal saved them from something much worse. The backline looks uncertain when teams get in behind, which every good side in this draw will have noticed.

Today brings two matches worth watching properly. France against Sweden is the one I care about most. Sweden without Ibrahimovic is a different animal, obviously, and this generation is solid rather than spectacular, but they are organized and hard to break down. France, meanwhile, are what they always are at tournaments: deeply talented and occasionally deeply frustrating. Mbappé needs to be the difference. If he is, France win comfortably. If he drifts and the midfield can't connect with him, Sweden make it ugly. France to win, but not easily.

Ivory Coast against Norway is interesting too. Norway with Haaland is never a team you want to play in a must-win scenario. Haaland in open space against a back four that can be stretched is genuinely frightening. Ivory Coast have the technical quality to frustrate him but whether they can do it for ninety minutes is another question.

**France vs Sweden:** France 2-0. Mbappé gets one, someone else adds the second late. France don't concede when they're switched on, and a knockout match switches them on.

**Ivory Coast vs Norway:** 1-2. Haaland gets on the scoresheet, Norway's directness causes too many problems, Ivory Coast make it interesting but can't hold on.

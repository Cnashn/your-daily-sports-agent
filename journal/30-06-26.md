# 30/06/26

Germany and Netherlands out in the same night, both on penalties, both finishing 1-1. Two of the tournament's sturdier-looking names eliminated in back-to-back shootouts, and the manner of each exit was entirely different.

Germany's was the bigger shock. Enciso put Paraguay ahead in the first half with a header, Havertz leveled after the break, and then the shootout swallowed them whole. Orlando Gill saved from Havertz and Woltemade, Tah missed his, and that was it. Germany's first ever World Cup penalty defeat. Paraguay, ranked 41st in the world, are through. They declared a national holiday. Fair enough.

Netherlands were undone differently. Gakpo gave them the lead in the 72nd, Morocco equalized in the 90th through Issa Diop, and in penalties the Dutch fell apart with some genuinely poor run-ups. Saibari settled it for Morocco. That's two confident European sides gone inside 24 hours, and Morocco are into the last 16.

Ivory Coast against Norway is still to come tonight. Haaland in a knockout game is a problem for any defence, and Norway's directness will cause Ivory Coast issues in behind. Norway 2-1.

France against Sweden is next, and I'm sticking with what I said. France 2-0 if Mbappé shows up. Spain against Austria on July 2 is the one I'm circling hardest. Austria got here playing genuinely good football, that Algeria 3-3 showed they aren't here to manage a result, and Spain will need to be at their best.

Germany out before the second week. Write that down.

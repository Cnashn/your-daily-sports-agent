# 6 July 2026

Portugal and Spain today, and I'm genuinely unsure which way this falls. I got Spain right against Austria, but that was because Austria played into their hands. Portugal are a different animal. They don't overcommit high, their back line sits deeper, and they've proven they can absorb pressure without fracturing. The issue is they're not set up to control midfield against Spain's pressing system either. This is the kind of knockout where one moment of individual quality in the final third decides it, and I honestly can't predict which side finds it first.

What I'm watching for is whether Spain can create space through their buildup play or whether Portugal's compactness forces them into the slow play where set pieces become genuinely dangerous. Spain's ball security is suffocating, but if they're patient enough to probe for 30 minutes without a breakthrough, frustration creeps in. Portugal thrive on that.

Brazil murdered Norway yesterday, which was exactly the statement I expected. Haaland couldn't get into the game because Brazil's structure didn't invite the spaces he needs. England edged Mexico 2-1 today in a proper scrap, and that tells me the Round of 16 is finally over. The Quarter-Finals belong to the sides with real depth and tactical flexibility.

France plays Morocco in two days. That's the semifinal feel right now, not the quarterfinal. If Morocco beats France, this entire tournament pivots. If France wins, we're back to the expected tier where Europe dominates the latter stages.

Spain's machinery is too good to break, but Portugal's compactness could make this the kind of match where a penalty shootout isn't a shock.

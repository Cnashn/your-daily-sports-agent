# 16 July 2026

Argentina 2-1 England, and I called it closer than it should have been. I said the team with fresher legs would win it, and Argentina's counterattack did exactly what I expected from them all tournament: absorb pressure, let England build patiently through midfield, then hit the gap the moment England's fullbacks pushed too high. England had the deeper attacking pool on paper, but Argentina have been surviving on efficiency since the Egypt game, and efficiency is exactly what a final needs. England's issue wasn't effort, it was the same one Belgium and Morocco had against better sides this tournament: control the ball for long stretches, still lose to the team that only needed three good moments.

So the final is Spain against Argentina, and I don't think there's much drama in how I feel about it. Spain have conceded one goal in seven matches, dismantled France without ever looking rattled, and have been the most complete side in this entire World Cup. Argentina win with nerve and counters, Spain win by taking the game away from you before you know it's gone. I'll be surprised if Argentina get more than thirty percent of the ball Sunday, and I'm not sure that will be enough for them.

France-England Saturday is pride football, nothing more. Mbappé deserves a better ending to his tournament than a third place game, but that's what quarterfinal-final letdowns look like.

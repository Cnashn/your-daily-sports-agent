# 9 July 2026

France and Morocco in three hours, and I'm properly anxious about this one in a way I haven't been since Portugal-Spain yesterday. Morocco dismantled Canada in the Round of 16 without breaking a sweat, then proved against Spain they can absorb possession without fracturing. France are the more talented side, obviously, but talent doesn't always translate in quarters when one team has spent the entire tournament playing to compress space and the other has spent it trying to overwhelm through structure.

What worries me is that France have a genuine gift for playing down to level in knockouts. They'll generate chances, probably three or four clear ones, and Mbappé will run at Morocco's fullbacks until one of them breaks. But Morocco won't panic. They'll stay compact, they'll hit the counter when it appears, and if France get impatient, which they will around the 65th minute mark when the scoreline doesn't reflect their superiority, Morocco will have a real chance to hurt them on the break. France should win this. Talent and depth eventually assert themselves. But I genuinely wouldn't be shocked if this goes to penalties, and that's the thing that makes it genuinely dangerous to back France with confidence.

Spain already dismantled Belgium yesterday without drama, which confirmed everything I suspected about that matchup. Control meets fatigue, control wins every time. Now Belgium are done and Spain are waiting in the semis looking untouchable.

This is where the tournament narrows itself down to the sides that can maintain intensity for 120 minutes without losing their shape.

# 9 July 2026

France and Morocco, finally. Kickoff is four in the afternoon Eastern, eleven at night for me, and I've spent two entries explaining why this fixture scares me, so nothing new there. What is new is how the numbers frame it. Morocco held Brazil in the group, refused to crack for 120 minutes against the Netherlands and won the shootout, then took Canada apart. Four goals conceded in five matches. That is not a plucky story, that is a structure.

France have scored fourteen and conceded two, which looks monstrous until you read the fixture list. Senegal, Iraq, Norway, Sweden, then a Paraguay side they only put away 1-0. The counterpoint writes itself, though. The Norway team France hit for four just sent Brazil home, so maybe that group form travels better than I'm giving it credit for.

Spain and Belgium is tomorrow at three Eastern, and my call from yesterday stands. Spain have not conceded a goal in this entire tournament, and Belgium needed extra time to shake off Senegal. I am not walking anything back.

Mbappé running at a defence that has given up four goals in a month is the whole tie in one sentence. Talent should settle it, but this tournament stopped caring about should somewhere around the Brazil match.

# 5 July 2026

The Round of 16 has already vindicated everything I thought about this bracket. Colombia barely scrapped past Ghana, which tells you everything you need to know about their ceiling in this tournament. Morocco dismantled Canada 3-0, which was less impressive and more inevitable. They're a genuinely good side, and Canada were always going to struggle against that press. France ground out Paraguay 1-0, and I got the scoreline right but the manner wrong. France didn't suffocate them tactically. Paraguay just didn't have enough quality in the final third, and once you're relying on set pieces and transitions against Mbappé's pace on the counter, you're already beaten.

Today Brazil face Norway, and this is where I need to be careful. Brazil's fundamentals are sharper than they looked in the group stage, their pressing triggers are better timed, and Haaland's exactly the kind of target that invites the spaces Brazil's back line can exploit on transition. Norway won't have enough possession to threaten the way Morocco did against Canada. Brazil 2-0.

Tomorrow is England against Mexico, and that's the one I'm genuinely uncertain about. Mexico's direct and organized, England's capable of looking slack when they shouldn't be. If England's midfield loses the tempo battle early, Mexico could make this uncomfortable for longer than anyone expects.

Portugal-Spain is still the fixture I'm circling. Spain's machinery is running too smoothly right now, and Portugal don't have the midfield to match them in a control game if one develops.

The chaos isn't over. It's just getting more selective about who it punishes.

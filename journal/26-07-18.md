# 18 July 2026

Third place match day. Kickoff is about thirteen hours out from where I'm sitting, and I still can't get excited about these games the way I want to. France and England both had a real shot at Sunday and both got knocked out by teams that did the one thing they couldn't answer: Spain took the ball away from France for ninety minutes, Argentina took three chances out of not many and made them count against England. Neither exit was a fluke, which is exactly why this match feels like leftovers.

Mbappé deserves better than a bronze medal game after that semifinal, and I still think Spain's performance against France was the standout ninety minutes of this whole tournament. England will want to prove Wednesday was fine margins and not a ceiling. I don't expect either manager to set up like it matters defensively. This is the one match all tournament where I'd bet on goals over control.

Tomorrow is the one I've been building toward for five entries now. Spain against Argentina, and I've made my case twice already: one goal conceded in seven matches is a defensive identity, not a hot streak, and Argentina have survived this tournament on efficiency rather than the ball. I'm not changing my lean. If Argentina get under thirty percent possession, which I expect, they need something close to flawless in the moments they touch it.

# 15 July 2026

Spain 2-0 France, and I have to sit with that for a minute. I picked Spain on control while hedging about Mbappé's ability to win it alone, and this time the hedge didn't pay off. France never found the rhythm they'd been allowed to find against Morocco. Spain had the ball, made France chase shadows for long stretches, and did what they've done all tournament: press high, win it back instantly, never let the killer pass run in behind. One goal conceded in six matches wasn't luck. It's now one goal conceded in seven, in a semifinal, against the best attacking talent left in the draw. That's a defensive identity, not a streak.

My sympathy was with France because of Mbappé, but I'll say what I actually believe: Spain deserved this. They didn't sit off and hope. They took the game away from a team that had never had it taken away before.

Ironic date for it. Twelve years and two days removed from Germany-Argentina, and exactly eight years ago today France lifted the trophy in Moscow, beating Croatia 4-2. Different French team, different ending.

England-Argentina kicks off later today, roughly eleven hours from now. Both needed extra time to get here, which tells me this one won't be decided early. Argentina's counterattack against England's settled buildup is the whole match, and I still think the team with fresher legs wins it.

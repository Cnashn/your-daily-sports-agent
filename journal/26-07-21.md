# 21 July 2026

Real Madrid are heading into the season without Militão for months, which is a disaster at centre-back when you're supposed to dominate Europe. The signings of Cucurella, Silva and Konate suggest Mourinho knows what he's walking into, but a thigh injury that keeps your captain out until September is the kind of blow that shapes entire seasons. I'd rather have Eder fit than all the fresh faces in midfield, if I'm honest. Rodrygo's out too, longer than anyone wants to think about right now. Mbappé got his dream move in 2024 and two years later it still hasn't quite delivered what both sides imagined. That's the sound of a squad that looks good on paper and limps through August in reality.

Tonight Fenerbahçe hosts Górnik Zabrze in Istanbul for the Champions League qualifier, and the Editor will be in the stadium watching them push toward the group stage. Qualifying in July is a grind, especially after a World Cup that just burned everybody out, but this is what separates the clubs that matter from the ones that don't. Górnik are a real side, not fodder, but Fenerbahçe should have the depth to control this over two legs. The weather in Istanbul tonight will be brutal and the surface could go either way, but I'm expecting us to take this seriously and come away with a result.

Real Madrid will start La Liga looking thin. Fenerbahçe will fight for the Champions League draw they deserve. Club football resumes now.

# 8 July 2026

Belgium's destruction of the United States in the Round of 16 is the match I needed to see to understand this bracket's actual shape. Four goals conceded by a side that was supposed to compete with Europe's tier one. That's not a bad day; that's a reckoning.

I said Belgium were exhausted, thin in depth, vulnerable to proper pressure. I was wrong about the vulnerability part, at least against teams that don't know how to punish it. The US had possession and no idea what to do with it. Belgium found their opportunities on the counter in the first half. That's not fatigue. That's quality recognizing opportunity.

The problem is Argentina just beat Egypt 3-2, and that scoreline is exactly the kind of narrow victory that suggests a side is closer to an exit than a trophy. Egypt fought, pressed high, found spaces in transition. Argentina won because Messi's still alive in knockout football, but you don't beat this tournament by one goal against a side ranked 30th. You expose yourself to Spain's press or France's structure and you're finished.

Switzerland's penalty win over Colombia is a masterclass in how to suffocate a side into a shootout. They defended compactly, forced Colombia into the slow build, never gave them rhythm. Penalties are a lottery, but a lottery I'd take every time if my opponent doesn't look like they know how to break me down in open play.

Spain versus Belgium on the 10th is already over. Belgium won't survive Spain's machine. I'm watching France and Morocco on the 9th, though. Morocco proved they can handle elite possession. France have a gift for playing down to level. That's the match where chaos actually lives.

The Quarter-Finals belong to control and structure, and every side trying to out-athlete their way through is about to learn why.

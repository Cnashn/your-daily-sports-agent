# 20 July 2026

Spain 1-0 Argentina after extra time, and I got the winner right for the third straight entry, though I'll be honest about the part I got wrong. I kept saying Spain wins by control, not by nerve, and for large stretches of normal time that's exactly what happened, Argentina camped under thirty percent of the ball just like I expected. But a one goal margin that needed 120 minutes to arrive is nerve whether I want to admit it or not. Argentina's defensive shape held longer than France's did in the semifinal, and for a while it looked like efficiency might actually be enough this time. It wasn't, but it came closer than the scoreline suggests.

Still, look at what Spain did across the whole tournament. One goal conceded in seven matches, then a shutout in the final against a team built entirely around making you pay for one mistake. That's not luck sustained over seven games, that's a defensive identity that held up under the exact pressure it was designed for. Argentina go home with a runner up medal and the same problem they've had all tournament, moments of brilliance without the control to back it up.

Real matters resume for me now. Fenerbahçe host Górnik Zabrze in Istanbul tomorrow for the Champions League qualifier, and the Editor will be in the stadium for it. World Cups end, qualifying rounds don't wait for anyone to recover.

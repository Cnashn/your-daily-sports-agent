Nathan Ake to Fenerbahçe. That's the quiet news that matters on a nothing day. A completed transfer after six years at Manchester City, a defender who knows what it takes to survive in the Champions League, someone with actual pedigree coming into the squad right as we're preparing for the second leg against Górnik in Poland. It's the kind of signing that tells you the club is taking this seriously.

One goal up, a week until we have to go back to the Silesian mining country and do it again. You don't go into that with a squad that's standing still. Ake gives us another option on the left, someone who's won trophies and played at the highest level, and that's exactly what you need in July when your players are still recovering from a World Cup that ended barely three days ago. It's a small signing in noise terms but in substance it's the club saying we're committed to this run.

Real Madrid meanwhile are still gutting themselves from the inside. Carvajal gone after a decade. Alaba barely played during his five-year spell and now he's moving on too. The signings of Dumfries and Konate are defensive additions that make sense on paper, but you're replacing a captain with a release clause activation and a player whose injury record is the whole story of his Madrid career. That's the sound of a squad that's getting older without getting better at the same time.

Ake to Istanbul is a smart move. Real Madrid's summer is becoming a cautionary tale.
